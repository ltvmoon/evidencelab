## Research Trees

Research Trees let you drill down into topics by building an explorable tree of sub-queries from your search results. Think of it as a guided deep-dive — each branch leads to more focused insights.

### How It Works

1. **Start from any search** — run a search and view the AI Summary at the top of the results.
2. **Drill into a sub-topic** — you have two ways to branch out:
   - Click the **"Find out more"** button next to any heading in the AI summary.
   - **Highlight any text** in the summary and click the popup button to research that specific phrase.
3. **Context builds automatically** — each sub-query inherits the root search query plus its parent topic, so results stay relevant as you go deeper.
4. **Navigate your tree** — a tree view panel lets you see all the branches you've explored. Click any previous node to jump back and review its results and summary.
5. **Filters carry forward** — all active filters (organization, date range, country, etc.) from the parent search are inherited by sub-queries, keeping your research scoped.
6. **Load previous research** — click **"Load Previous Research"** at the top of the AI summary to restore a tree from an earlier session.

### Use Cases

* **Literature reviews** — start with a broad topic like "food security in East Africa" and systematically drill into specific aspects (agricultural productivity, climate adaptation, nutritional outcomes).
* **Gap analysis** — explore related sub-topics to quickly identify where evidence is strong and where it's lacking.
* **Evidence synthesis** — build a structured overview of a complex topic by exploring multiple branches, each with its own AI summary and source documents.
* **Preparing briefings** — use the tree to organize background research, with each node providing a focused summary on a specific aspect.
